# Lean Learning Notes

This repository contains my personal notes and exercises for learning the Lean theorem prover.

I am a third-year undergraduate student majoring in Statistics at the University of Science and Technology of China. I am interested in mathematical formalization, probability theory, statistics, and the interaction between Lean and modern mathematics.

## Goals

The main goals of this repository are:

- To record my learning process of Lean.
- To practice reading and writing Lean theorem statements.
- To understand basic type theory, propositions, tactics, and proofs.
- To gradually move toward formalizing probability and statistics-related results.

## Contents

- `notes/`: Markdown notes on Lean syntax, logic, type theory, tactics, and mathematical formalization.
- `exercises/`: Lean exercise files organized by topics or weeks.
- `references/`: Useful links and references for learning Lean.

## Current Learning Plan

I am currently learning Lean through:

- Basic Lean syntax and theorem statements.
- Propositions as types.
- Tactic-based proofs.
- Reading examples from Mathlib and formalization projects.
- Studying the formalization pipeline of mathematical results.
